Testimonies

This section gathers texts in which Olmo Gómez Aldaz speaks from lived experience. They are interventions, statements and testimonial pieces connected with identity, adoption, filiation, stolen babies, democratic memory and the abolition of adoption.

The texts gathered here form part of a public trajectory of identity restitution. They arise from a life marked by identity abduction, irregular adoption, the search for origin, the recovery of biological filiation, judicially recognised dual filiation and recognition as a victim within the framework of democratic memory.

Testimony has a documentary place here. It fixes facts, preserves memory, orders experience and leaves a public record of what was lived. Each text preserves a specific moment of public speech: a conference, an intervention, a statement or a public position connected with the recovery of origin and the restitution of identity.

Testimonial line

The testimonial line gathers texts built from first-person experience, lived memory and public speech. Its centre is direct experience: what happened, what was recovered, what was documented and what was said before others.

In cases of irregular adoption, stolen babies, identity abduction and concealment of origin, the harm is often scattered across files, silences, family narratives, incomplete archives and interested versions. Testimony brings those pieces together through the voice of the person who lived their consequences.

These texts explain what it means to grow up inside an imposed identity, search for origin, find the truth, sustain it before others and defend it before institutions, families, registries and social discourses.

Personal experience appears linked to documents, genetic evidence, judicial rulings, institutional recognition and critical reflection. Testimony thus becomes a form of continuity between life, document, justice and memory.

Published testimonies

The testimonies published in this section gather interventions and texts written from a lived experience of adoption, identity abduction, recovery of origin and restitution of biological filiation.

Each publication preserves its context: title, date, place, public framework, documentary version and links to the repositories where it has been archived. The website gathers these materials in a stable, accessible and indexable structure.

The texts published in this line address adoption through its concrete effects: birth identity, filiation, dual filiation, stolen babies, democratic memory, recovery of origin, adoptive harm and the abolition of adoption.

This page works as an index of the testimonies available within the public work of Olmo Gómez Aldaz.

Purpose of this section

This section preserves texts where lived experience occupies the centre of the document. Testimonial speech makes it possible to understand how facts recorded in documents affect an entire life: name, surnames, registry, family belonging, health, bonds and the possibility of knowing who one is.

In this line, the testimonies of Olmo Gómez Aldaz form part of a public work on identity, filiation, origin, adoption, memory and restitution. They name the violence of substitution, the difficulty of recovering origin, the cost of sustaining a denied truth and the need to abolish an institution that can erase identity and manufacture filiations through a legal fiction.

The unity of this section lies in the position from which the texts are written: lived experience converted into public, documented and accessible speech.
