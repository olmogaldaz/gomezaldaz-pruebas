Work > Adoption

Adoption

This section brings together critical texts by Olmo Gómez Aldaz on adoption, identicide, abolition of identicide, erasure of origin, institutional violence, documentary destruction of filiation and registry stigma.

It includes the Undoing Adoption line of work, devoted to dismantling adoption as an institution of substitution, erasure of origins, identity violence and documentary destruction of filiation.

The texts published in this section form part of a conceptual and political elaboration on full adoption, closed adoption, identicide, the abolition of adoption and its legal, symbolic and registry effects.

Line of work

Undoing Adoption functions here as a critical line within the published work: not as a separate folder, but as a conceptual framework for thinking adoption from the harm it produces, not from the narrative that justifies it.

The common axis of these texts is the critique of adoption as a practice of identity substitution, concealment of origin, genealogical rupture, symbolic violence and State administration of filiation.

Conceptual order

The recommended reading order is: Identicide, Abolition of Identicide, Aboliphobia, Adoption as colonial identicide genocide and The registry stigma of adoption.

This order moves from the central concept to the abolitionist framework, then to the defensive reaction against that critical thought and, finally, to two specific developments: the colonial-biopolitical and the registry-legal dimensions.
